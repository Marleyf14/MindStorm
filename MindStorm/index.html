Geomagnetic Storms Education

Here you will learn about geomagnetic storms, a fascinating phenomenon that occurs in space but directly affects Earth! Here, you will understand in a simple and engaging way how these storms occur, what events on the Sun are necessary to trigger these bursts of energy and the impact they can have on our planet, such as beautiful auroras or even problems with satellites and power grids. If you are a student curious about the universe and want to learn more about the mysteries of space and how it influences our daily lives, this is the right place!

Geomagnetic
Storms

May 2024 has already proven to be a particularly stormy month for our Sun. During the first full week of May, a barrage of large solar flares and coronal mass ejections (CMEs) launched clouds of charged particles and magnetic fields toward Earth, creating the strongest solar storm to reach Earth in two decades — and possibly one of the strongest displays of auroras on record in the past 500 years.

Space
Weather

Space weather refers to the environmental conditions in space caused primarilly by the Sun's activity, such as solar winds and geomagnetic storms. These phenomena, driven by solar events like solar flares and coronal mass ejections (CMEs), can impact planets, moons, and space missions across the Solar System.
